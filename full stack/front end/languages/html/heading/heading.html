HTML Heading Tag

What is Heading Tag ?


In HTML, heading tags are used to define headings or titles within a document. Headings are hierarchical, meaning that they come in different levels, and the level of the heading indicates its importance and relationship to other headings.

In this example, h1 is the main heading, h2 is a subheading under h1, and h3 is a subheading under h2. The hierarchical structure provides a visual representation of the document's organization and helps with accessibility and search engine optimization. Additionally, the browser may use these headings to create an outline of the document.

Example


<h1>Heading 1 </h1>
<h2>Heading 2 </h2>
<h3>Heading 3 </h3>
<h4>Heading 4 </h4>
<h5>Heading 5 </h5>
<h6>Heading 6 </h6>

Output


Heading 1


Heading 2


Heading 3


Heading 4


Heading 5

Heading 6
